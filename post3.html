Choosing Your Career Direction

⇣



October 20 2021

Does carpe diem brings you more excitement than anxiety?

Carpe diem or seize the day is a Latin phrase that urges people to make most of the present and have less regards to the future.

Recently, I related with this a lot more. Ever since I was younger, I had a dream of becoming a doctor. It wasn't until of late that I realized that it wasn't a career that I felt truly passionate about.

So why was I stuck for 10+ years stuck onto this dream? That question took me some time to understand where its origin starts from the beginning of how I was inspired to become one. When I was a lot younger, there was a project that we had to do of finding a career to pursue. As a second generation of immigrants - since my parents were the first - the careers I've seen was quite limited. Out of the limited list, being a doctor seemed the most appealing.

It was the most appealing for several reasons, (1) my parents saw it as the most stable job for income, (2) the career seems to carry some prestige, (3) I cared about people and want to help them out and (4) due to some neglect from my parents, the only person that seemed to care for me was a doctor. As you might see that out of all of the reasons, only the last one 2 were personal but it was not because I wanted to do it for me. Instead, I wanted to become a doctor to thank that person for their care. I was also obsessed thinking that if I become a doctor, I will be just like them personality-wise. She is meticious, quick-witted, driven and resilient. I wanted that. In summary: I idolized and wrongfully equated those qualities with the career.

The desire of having those traits did somewhat manifest but I quickly realized that it was the activities that built me as I am rather than having to be in that career. I found that the most important to carpe diem is to pick a career that can execute your strengths the most as well as challenge you to grow along with your weaknesses. I realized that I actually enjoy meeting people, traveling, strategizing and planning. I'm also a curious and innovative person that loves problem-solving via technology. Further thinking about the possbilities through surfing the web, I realized that my strengths can truly thrive in marketing and sales.

There's always things to learn about other people and the products can be sold creatively to customers, eventually, improving their projects. I find that exciting. Letting go a dream that felt wrong really allowed me to proceed into something suiting.

It made me thought that perhaps carpe diem would mainly bring anxiety when a goal doesn't align with personal values. When the goal does align with personal values, it can bring in a lot more confidence.

Hope this helps you if you are still figuring out your career direction.
